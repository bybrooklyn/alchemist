# AI Usage Policy

Alchemist welcomes contributors who use AI tools thoughtfully. AI can be a
helpful part of research, brainstorming, writing, and development, but every
contribution should ultimately be understood, reviewed, and supported by the
person submitting it.

## Guidelines for Contributors

- **Please disclose AI assistance.** Let us know which tool or tools you used
  (for example, Claude Code, Cursor, or Codex) and how they contributed to the
  work. A brief note is enough; the goal is transparency, not judgment.

- **Understand your changes.** Before submitting code, make sure you can
  explain what it does, why it is needed, and how it fits into the rest of the
  system. If you are still learning, that is completely okay—ask questions
  and use the review process as an opportunity to learn.

- **Review and edit AI-assisted writing.** AI may be used to help draft issues,
  discussions, and pull requests, but a human should carefully review and
  edit the final text before submitting it. Please check the facts, remove
  unnecessary detail, and make sure the result reflects your own intent.

- **Submit human-created media.** AI-generated art, images, video, audio, and
  similar media are not accepted as project contributions. Text and code may
  be AI-assisted when they follow the guidelines above.

- **Prioritize quality and respect.** Contributions that are confusing,
  inaccurate, or difficult to review may be closed or sent back for revision,
  regardless of whether AI was involved. We will explain what needs attention
  where possible, and we encourage contributors to learn from the feedback.

These guidelines apply to outside contributions to Alchemist. Maintainers may
use AI tools at their discretion and are responsible for applying the same
standards of care, review, and judgment to their work.

## There Are Humans Here

Please remember that Alchemist is maintained by people. Every discussion,
issue, and pull request is read and reviewed by humans. These are points where people collaborate, and
submissions that are thoughtful, relevant, and easy to understand help keep
that collaboration welcoming and sustainable.

AI tools can produce useful work, but they can also introduce mistakes,
unnecessary detail, or assumptions that are easy to miss. The person using the
tool is responsible for checking the result and for making sure it is ready to
share. That responsibility is not a barrier to using AI—it is part of using it
well.

## AI Is Welcome Here

Alchemist is written with plenty of AI assistance, and many maintainers use AI
tools as part of their workflow. We welcome AI as a tool when it is used with
care, transparency, and accountability.

This policy is intended to protect maintainers and make collaboration easier,
not to discourage people from using AI or to judge how they work. If you are
new to the project or to the technology, you are welcome here. Please ask
questions, be open about what you know and what you are still figuring out,
and use feedback to improve your contribution.

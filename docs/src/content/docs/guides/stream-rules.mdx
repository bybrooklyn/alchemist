"Streaming Rules" are special settings Alchemist uses to make sure your videos play smoothly on any device, from a giant 4K TV to a small smartphone over a weak Wi-Fi connection.

## Why do we need Streaming Rules?

The goal is to make your video files:
- **Compatible:** They should play on almost anything (TVs, phones, tablets).
- **Smooth:** They shouldn't "buffer" or stutter when you're watching them.
- **Vibrant:** Colors should look natural, even on older screens.

## Smart Bitrate Management

Alchemist doesn't just use one setting for everything. It looks at the **Resolution** of your video and adjusts:
- **4K Videos:** Needs more data to keep that "wow" factor.
- **1080p Videos:** Optimized for a crisp, clean look on most TVs.
- **SD (Standard Definition):** Shrinks the file as much as possible without making it look "blocky."

## HDR to SDR (Tonemapping)

If you have modern 4K movies with "HDR" (High Dynamic Range), they can look "washed out" or gray on older TVs. Alchemist can automatically fix this through a process called **Tonemapping**. It recalculates the colors so they look bright and colorful on any screen.

### Which algorithm should I pick?
- **Hable:** Great all-around choice for natural colors.
- **Mobius:** Good if you want to keep the "pop" of the original colors.
- **Reinhard:** A classic choice that works well for most movies.

## Subtitles that Work Everywhere

Some devices (like old smart TVs) struggle to show subtitles. Alchemist has a "Burn" mode that permanently draws the subtitles onto the video image. This way, the subtitles will **always** work, no matter how old the device is.
